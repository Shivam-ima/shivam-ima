Tribute to Major Mohit Sharma

Biography

Major Mohit Sharma was born on 13th January 1978 in Rohtak, Haryana. He was a courageous and dedicated officer of the Indian Army, serving with the 1st Battalion, Parachute Regiment (1 PARA SF), India's elite Special Forces unit.

He was commissioned into the Indian Army on 11th December 1999, and his career was marked by numerous successful operations and an unwavering commitment to his nation. As a part of the Special Forces, Major Sharma was involved in high-risk missions that demanded exceptional skill, leadership, and bravery.

On 27th March 2009, Major Sharma was killed in action during a counter-insurgency operation in Kupwara district, Jammu and Kashmir. Despite being severely injured, he continued to lead his men and ensured the success of the mission. His sacrifice is a testament to his courage, dedication, and patriotism.

For his bravery and supreme sacrifice, Major Mohit Sharma was posthumously awarded the Ashoka Chakra, India's highest peacetime gallantry award.
